What you’re seeing is basically a “round-trip movement of money” inside Touch ’n Go eWallet’s GO+ system, not extra charges.

Let’s break your pattern down:

1. What “GO+ Balance” actually is

GO+ (inside Touch 'n Go eWallet) is a cash management / investment wallet.

So money in GO+ is not your normal eWallet balance — it’s parked in a fund (money market fund) that earns small daily returns.

2. Why you see TWO transactions every time
Example you showed:
+MYR 25.00 → Quick Reload Payment (via GO+Balance)
-MYR 25.00 → CELCOM MOBILE SDN BHD

This usually means:

Step A — Money is pulled from GO+

When you pay something (like Celcom bill), the system first:

takes money out of GO+ balance

That shows as:

Quick Reload Payment (via GO+Balance)
Step B — Immediately used for payment

Then the money is:

sent to the merchant (Celcom)
or routed via internal settlement

So you see:

Merchant deduction (Celcom / Fund Transfer / etc.)
3. Why it looks like “double entries”

It’s not double spending. It’s just internal accounting split into two legs:

Step	Meaning
+ GO+ Quick Reload Payment	Money leaving investment fund into eWallet system
- Merchant / Transfer	Money leaving to pay the actual bill
4. Why “Fund Transfer” sometimes appears instead of merchant

Some payments don’t go directly to the merchant system. Instead:

GO+ → eWallet main balance / clearing account
then → merchant

So it shows up as:

Fund Transfer
5. Why GO+ is involved at all

Because your settings likely have:

GO+ set as default funding source, or
“auto top-up from GO+ when paying”

So instead of using your normal eWallet balance, it:

automatically pulls from GO+ first

6. When this is normal vs not normal

✅ Normal:

Quick Reload Payment + matching debit right after
Same amount both sides
Happens during bills / QR / transfers

🚨 Not normal (worth checking):

Extra unknown deductions
GO+ withdrawals you didn’t trigger
mismatched amounts

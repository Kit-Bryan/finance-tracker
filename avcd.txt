Yes, based on what you've learned about GO+, that note is likely backwards.

You currently have:

Date	Description	Amount
23 Oct	MRT Bandar Utama	-MYR 10.00
23 Oct	Reload to GO+ balance / Quick Reload Payment (via GO+Balance)	+MYR 10.00

The phrase "Reload to GO+ balance" suggests:

eWallet → GO+

But "Quick Reload Payment (via GO+Balance)" actually means:

GO+ → eWallet

to fund the MRT payment.

So a more accurate interpretation would be:

Transaction	Meaning
+MYR 10.00 Quick Reload Payment (via GO+Balance)	GO+ funded the eWallet with RM10
-MYR 10.00 MRT Bandar Utama	eWallet paid RM10 to MRT

In other words, the +MYR 10.00 is not money going into GO+. It's money coming out of GO+ and temporarily appearing in the eWallet before being spent.

For your finance tracker, I'd either:

Ignore the Quick Reload Payment (via GO+Balance) completely, or
Relabel it as something like:
"GO+ → eWallet funding"
"GO+ withdrawal for payment"
"Internal GO+ funding"

But I would not label it "Reload to GO+ balance", because that's the opposite direction of the money flow.
